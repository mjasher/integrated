calcIrrigationEfficiency(crop_water_req_ML_per_Ha)[source]¶

In this model, irrigation efficiency is an assumed/known value. With breakeven analysis we vary this value to determine a point of concern. This method is a stub which should be overwritten by implementing Classes.

In the interest of context:

Percentage of water delivered to the field that is used beneficially (Irrigation Efficiency - \(E_{i}\)) can be calculated as

\(E_{i} = 100(W_{b} / W_{f})\), where \(W_{b}\) is Water used beneficially, and \(W_{f}\) is water delivered to field

as seen in Rogers et al. (1997)

Parameters:crop_water_req_ML_per_Ha (float, ML per Hectare) – Amount of water in ML per Ha applied to the field.
Returns:percent (e.g. 76 percent = 0.76 * 100 = 76)
Return type:numeric

calcWaterApplicationEfficiency(water_delivered_to_field)[source]¶

Context: Rogers et al. (1997)

Percentage of water delivered to the field used by the crop

\(E_{a} = 100(W_{c} / W_{f})\), where \(E_{a}\) is Water Application Efficiency

\(W_{c}\) is water available for use by the crop

\(W_{f}\) is the water delivered to the field

Parameters:water_delivered_to_field (numeric, MegaLitres) – Amount of water (in ML) applied to the field
Returns:percentage (e.g. 0.76 = 76 percent = 76)
Return type:numeric
